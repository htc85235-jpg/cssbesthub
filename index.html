What is CSS?

The Civil Superior Services Examination, explained

Conducted annually by the Federal Public Service Commission (FPSC), the CSS exam is Pakistan's most prestigious competitive exam — the gateway to 12 occupational groups that administer, defend, and represent the nation.

CONDUCTED BY FPSC

Federal Public Service Commission

FPSC is the federal agency mandated under Article 242 of the Constitution to recruit civil servants for the Government of Pakistan. It conducts the CSS exam every February across 21 examination centres nationwide.

Did you know?

The CSS exam was first held in 1926 during British rule as the “Indian Civil Service Exam”. Today, the pass rate hovers around 2% — making it one of the toughest competitive exams in the world.

Eligibility Criteria

Education

Bachelor's degree (14 years of education) from an HEC-recognized university with at least 2nd division.

Age Limit

Minimum 21 years and maximum 30 years on 31st December of the exam year (relaxation applies for some categories).

Nationality

Pakistani citizen (male/female). Azad Kashmir nationals and state subjects of GB/FATA also eligible per rules.

Attempts

Maximum 3 attempts for regular candidates. Documented proof of all claims required at application stage.

3-Stage Selection

The CSS Exam Structure

A rigorous three-stage selection process designed to identify Pakistan's future administrators, diplomats, and police officers. Each stage filters candidates further — only the best reach the final merit list.

01

Written Examination

February • ~2 weeks1200 Marks

The first and most decisive stage. 12 papers split across 6 compulsory subjects and 6 optional papers (from groups you choose). Each paper is 100 marks, 3 hours duration. English Essay and Islamiat have minimum 40% qualifying threshold; all others need 33%.

  • 6 Compulsory Papers (600 marks)
  • 6 Optional Papers (600 marks)
  • Minimum 40% in Essay & Islamiat
  • Minimum 33% in every other paper
  • Aggregate 40% required to qualify
02

Psychological Assessment

May–June • 2 daysQualifying

Conducted by a panel of psychologists at designated centres. Includes personality tests, group discussions, command tasks, intelligence tests, and in-depth interviews. Assesses leadership potential, decision-making, and mental resilience under pressure.

  • Written psychological tests
  • Group discussions & command tasks
  • Individual psychological interview
  • Personality & leadership assessment
  • No marks — only qualifying
03

Viva Voce (Interview)

June–July • 30 min300 Marks

The final stage before the merit list. A panel headed by an FPSC member quizzes you on optional subjects, current affairs, Pakistan affairs, and your hobbies. Tests your confidence, presence of mind, communication skills, and overall personality.

  • 300 marks added to written total
  • Conducted at FPSC regional offices
  • Tests communication & personality
  • Optional subjects carry weightage
  • Final merit = Written + Viva
Written→Psychological→Viva Voce→Final Merit

One-Year Cycle

Exam Schedule & Timeline

From online application to final merit list — the entire CSS cycle spans around 9 months. Mark your calendar carefully; missing a single FPSC deadline can cost you an entire attempt.

September–October

Online Application

FPSC opens the online application portal. Submit forms via fpsc.gov.pk with required documents, fees, and subject selections. Late submissions are not entertained.

Mid–Late October

Admission Certificates

FPSC issues Roll Number slips with examination centre, paper schedule, and candidate details. Check the FPSC website to download.

Mid-February

Written Exam Begins

Written exam starts mid-February and runs for ~2 weeks. Two papers daily — morning (9 AM–12 PM) and afternoon (2 PM–5 PM) at 21 centres nationwide.

March–April

Result Declaration

FPSC evaluates answer sheets for ~3–4 months. Written result is typically declared in March–April of the following year, posted on FPSC website.

May–June

Psychological Assessment

Qualified candidates receive calls for psychological assessment at designated centres. Personality tests, group discussions, command tasks, and individual interviews.

June–July

Viva Voce & Final Result

Final viva voce interview at FPSC regional offices. Final merit list (Written + Viva) declared in June–July. Recommended candidates join CSA Lahore for training.

Never miss a deadline

Official FPSC notifications are published at fpsc.gov.pk. Bookmark the site and check weekly during application season.

Strategy & Materials

Preparation Playbook

Hard work without direction is wasted effort. Here are the time-tested strategies, book lists, and a sample daily routine that have produced toppers year after year.

Build a 6-Month Plan

Divide prep into 4 phases: Foundation (3 months) → Practice (2 months) → Revision (1 month) → Mock tests (last 3 weeks). Always work backward from the exam date.

8–10 Hours Daily

Quality beats quantity. Three focused 90-minute deep-work blocks beat 10 distracted hours. Use Pomodoro: 50 min study + 10 min break.

Write Daily

CSS is a writing exam, not a reading exam. Write at least 500 words daily — essays, answers, notes. Hand-writing builds stamina for 3-hour papers.

Read Dawn Daily

Dawn newspaper is non-negotiable. Read editorial, op-ed, international, and business pages. Make notes of facts, quotes, and arguments.

Make Notes, Not Highlights

Highlighting creates the illusion of learning. Convert highlights into your own handwritten notes — that's where real retention happens.

Solve Past 10 Years

Past papers are your best guide. Solve at least 10 years of past papers for every subject — patterns repeat more often than you think.
